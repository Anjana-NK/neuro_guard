# Academic and Workplace Accommodations for Autistic Individuals in India

This document describes the legally mandated and recommended accommodations for autistic individuals in educational institutions and workplace environments in India.

## Academic Accommodations

Under the guidelines issued by the Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment and the Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) for students with benchmark disabilities:

### Key Entitlements
1. **Extra Time**: Autistic students are entitled to compensatory time during exams. Typically, this is 20 minutes extra per hour of examination (e.g., 60 minutes extra for a 3-hour exam).
2. **Scribe / Amanuensis Support**: If the student has writing or motor coordination issues, they can request a government-approved scribe to write the answers. The student can also choose to write using a computer/keyboard with spell-check and predictive options disabled.
3. **Exemptions**: 
   - Third language exemption: Exempted from studying a third language up to Class 10.
   - Alternate subjects: Can choose vocational subjects or practical-oriented courses in place of standard theory-heavy courses.
4. **Relaxed Pass Criteria**: Many state universities and schools offer flexible grading or lower minimum marks for admission.

### How to Request
- Submit a request to the school principal or university registrar at the start of the academic year.
- Attach the official UDID card or a certificate showing autism spectrum disorder diagnosis.
- Specify the exact accommodations needed (e.g., extra time, separate quiet examination room to prevent sensory overload).

## Workplace Accommodations

Corporate accommodations support neurodivergent employees under Section 34 and 35 of the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (RPwD) Act 2016 which mandates equal opportunity policies in public and private institutions:

### Key Accommodations
1. **Sensory Accommodations**:
   - Noise-canceling headphones allowed in the workplace.
   - Low-stimulation quiet rooms or sensory booths.
   - Adjustable dimmable lighting in the workstation area.
   - Desk placement away from high-traffic hallways or noisy equipment (e.g., printers, kitchen).
2. **Flexible Work Arrangements**:
   - Hybrid work schedules or remote work options.
   - Flexible starting and finishing hours to avoid peak rush-hour transit which triggers sensory fatigue.
   - Frequent structured micro-breaks to rest and recharge.
3. **Structured Communication**:
   - Clear written instructions instead of ambiguous verbal assignments.
   - Standard task checklists and detailed project timelines.
   - Visual step-by-step documentation.
4. **Mentorship & Support**:
   - Designating a workplace buddy or neurodivergence champion to assist with social navigation.
   - Educating the immediate team/managers through sensory sensitivity awareness programs.
