# Swavlamban Card (UDID) application guidance

The Unique Disability ID (UDID) card, also known as the Swavlamban Card, is a initiative by the Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment, Government of India. It aims to build a national database for persons with disabilities (PwDs) and issue a single, verified card that serves as identification and eligibility verification for government schemes.

## Eligibility
- Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is officially recognized under the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (RPwD) Act 2016.
- Individuals diagnosed with Autism can apply for the UDID card, regardless of age.
- Requires assessment and certification by a designated medical board.

## How to Apply
1. Register online at the official portal: swavlambancard.gov.in
2. Fill out the application form with personal details, educational history, employment status, and disability details.
3. Upload required documents:
   - Recent passport-size photograph.
   - Address proof (Aadhaar card, voter ID, passport, or utility bill).
   - Identity proof (Aadhaar, PAN, etc.).
   - Previous disability certificate (if already issued by a government hospital).
4. Select the nearest government hospital/medical board for assessment.
5. Visit the hospital on the scheduled date for physical/clinical assessment. The medical board consisting of psychiatrists, pediatricians, and clinical psychologists will verify the diagnosis and compute the disability percentage.
6. Once approved, the UDID card and digital certificate can be downloaded online, and a physical card will be printed and couriered to the registered address.

## Key Benefits
- Simplifies access to federal and state disability pension grants.
- Provides travel concessions in Indian Railways (up to 75% off for the cardholder and one escort) and state bus transport.
- Simplifies applications for academic scholarships and vocational training support.
- Accepted as valid proof of identity and disability across all government departments and institutions in India.
